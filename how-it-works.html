Simple step flow

From court booking to a closed game, Padel-Up keeps the organizer in control while every player receives clear requests, approvals, and consent notifications.

Step 1

Organizer books the court

The first player acts as the organizer and books the court outside Padel-Up, using the club or booking app to choose the location, time, court, and duration.

Step 2

Organizer creates the game

The organizer defines the game details: date, time, duration, club location, desired player gender and rating, and optional invited players. Padel-Up notifies all current game players when the game is created or updated.

Step 3

Another player asks to join

A player searches for open games by filtering locations, rating ranges, and calendar availability. They review the matching games, choose one, and send a join request.

Step 4

Organizer approves or rejects

The organizer receives a join request notification for the missing player slot. The link opens the pending approvals list, where the organizer can approve or reject the request. In both cases, the player is notified.

Step 5

Player gives final consent

If the organizer adds a player directly, that player receives a consent-pending notification. The link opens the waiting-for-consent list, where the player can approve or reject the invitation. The organizer is notified either way.

Step 6

The game closes automatically

When the fourth player is approved, the game closes automatically and every confirmed player receives a game-closed message. Any remaining candidates are removed from the closed game, and their other active requests are deactivated and archived.
